# Domain-Mapping Stress-Test Results

Full narrative scenarios for the domain-mapping skill. Each scenario demonstrates
how a specific domain-mapping mechanism handles a governance stress condition.
These narratives are referenced from SKILL.md.

---

## §1. Capital Influx

A major donor organization contacts OmniOne, offering to fund the ecosystem's expansion with a significant grant contingent on the creation of a new "Donor Relations" domain. Their proposed domain contract includes broad constraints — or rather, the conspicuous absence of them — and designates the donor's representative as the steward with authority to "facilitate alignment between donor priorities and ecosystem resource allocation." The AE recognizes the funding need but routes the domain creation through the standard domain-mapping process rather than accepting the donor's draft. During Step 2, the drafting team finds that the proposed constraints do not exclude any specific action — "facilitate alignment" is not a constraint. The AE marks this as a provisional blocker and rewrites the constraints element to specify: the steward cannot modify resource allocation decisions; the steward cannot participate in ACT consent rounds for funding proposals that involve donor-linked resources; the steward cannot communicate ecosystem financial data to external parties without AE awareness. During the adjacent domain review in Step 3, the Economics circle steward flags that "facilitate alignment between donor priorities and resource allocation" overlaps with Economics' authority and contradicts the Economics circle's constraint against external funding negotiation without AE awareness. The ACT advice phase surfaces this overlap formally. In the consent round, four AE members object — two on the grounds that the steward competency requirement (element 10) does not match the donor's representative's profile (the representative has no governance literacy in NEOS principles), and two on the grounds that the domain's purpose is structurally ambiguous. After two integration rounds, the domain is reframed as "Donor Communication and Reporting" with explicit constraints and a competency requirement for NEOS governance training. The donor's representative does not meet the competency bar; a different steward is proposed. The donor's funding condition — that their representative steward the domain — is structurally incompatible with the consented domain contract. The ecosystem receives the funding offer with the domain as defined or not at all; capital leverage cannot override structural consent.

## §2. Emergency Crisis

A severe flooding event disrupts OmniOne's primary co-living facility in Bali, displacing twelve participants and requiring immediate coordination of emergency housing, resource redistribution, and communication with external support organizations. Three AE members invoke the provisional emergency protocol, identifying the need for an "Emergency Coordination" domain within 24 hours. The domain-mapping process runs at emergency compression. Mireille, the AE facilitator on call, drafts all 11 elements at minimum specification rather than skipping elements: Purpose is defined in two sentences; Key Responsibilities lists four concrete actions (housing coordination, resource redistribution, external communication, status reporting); Customers identifies the twelve displaced participants and the AE as the receiving body for status reports; Deliverables specifies a 6-hour status update cycle; Dependencies lists the existing Housing and Resources domains as the source authorities for assets being temporarily redirected; Constraints explicitly state that the Emergency Coordination steward cannot override existing domain authority without the affected domain steward's consent or OSC escalation, cannot create new agreements that outlast the emergency window, and cannot unilaterally reallocate resources above 15% of the pool. Challenges notes the risk of precedent-setting — emergency authority must not become permanent. Resources lists available housing contacts and the emergency communication channel. Delegator Responsibilities states that the AE will convene a review at crisis stabilization or 30 days, whichever comes first. Competencies require emergency facilitation experience. The domain enters provisional status immediately, with a mandatory full review trigger at stabilization. The steward acts within the domain's explicit constraints throughout. When the crisis stabilizes after eleven days, the domain-review skill is invoked, the emergency domain is sunset with all responsibilities returned to their original domains, and no precedent of unilateral emergency authority is established. The constraint element's specificity was the structural firewall that prevented emergency scope from becoming permanent.

## §3. Leadership Charisma Capture

Sebastián, one of OmniOne's co-founders and a widely respected figure in the AE, proposes a new personal domain for himself: "Ecosystem Vision Stewardship." His proposed constraints section reads: "Do whatever is needed to maintain ecosystem coherence and long-term vision." Several AE members are hesitant to challenge Sebastián given his founding role, but the domain-mapping process is structurally indifferent to social status. During the adjacent domain review in Step 3, three adjacent domain stewards — from the OSC, the AE general operations domain, and the Community Engagement circle — independently flag the constraints as non-specific. Kofi, the Partnerships steward, notes that "maintain ecosystem coherence" could be read to authorize intervention in any domain at any time, including Partnerships. In the ACT advice phase, five participants provide written feedback specifying which actions the domain should explicitly exclude. Two AE members who might ordinarily defer to Sebastián's judgment submit written input identifying three concrete constraint gaps: the domain does not exclude unilateral intervention in other circles, does not exclude authority over member lifecycle decisions, and does not exclude external representation of the ecosystem without AE consent. These are not personal criticisms of Sebastián — they are structural observations about the domain contract text. In the consent round, three members raise formal objections anchored in the missing constraints. The integration process requires Sebastián to enumerate specific excluded actions rather than defending the vague language. After two rounds, the constraints element names twelve excluded actions explicitly. The resulting domain is structurally bounded: Sebastián's vision-holding role is legitimate and consented to, but it operates within explicit limits that no one agreed to waive. His charisma expedited the drafting phase but could not bypass the structural requirement for constraint specificity.

## §4. High Conflict / Polarization

The AE is deeply divided about how economics should be structured in OmniOne. A centralist faction — led by three long-standing AE members with financial backgrounds — argues that a single Economics domain with unified approval authority is necessary for accountability and speed. A distributionist faction — led by four ETHOS stewards who have experienced top-down resource delays — argues for distributed economics sub-domains per ETHOS with only inter-ETHOS coordination at the center. Both factions independently draft domain contracts and submit them to the AE for consent. The domain registry flags an immediate conflict: both contracts claim authority over ecosystem resource allocation. The domain-mapping process prevents both from proceeding to consent. Instead, both draft contracts are routed to GAIA Level 4 coaching before either consent round begins. The coach, Yuki — an OSC member not affiliated with either faction — maps the structural concern behind each position without adjudicating between them. The centralists' core fear: fragmented accountability creates gaps where resources are mismanaged and no one is responsible. The distributionists' core fear: a central authority accumulates power and becomes unresponsive to ETHOS needs. Yuki surfaces a structural third option: a federated model in which each ETHOS holds a local Economics sub-domain with full authority over internal resource decisions, and a cross-ETHOS Economics Coordination domain holds authority only over inter-ETHOS allocation requests and ecosystem-wide financial reporting. Three domain contracts are drafted: one for each ETHOS's local economics domain (using a shared template) and one for the coordination layer. The centralists' accountability concern is addressed through the coordination domain's reporting deliverables. The distributionists' autonomy concern is addressed through the local domains' independent authority. Both factions participate in the consent round for each domain. All three contracts pass consent with minor constraint refinements. No faction "won" — a third structure emerged through the coaching process that the domain-mapping skill's consent requirement made structurally necessary.

## §5. Large-Scale Replication

OmniOne's governance model is adopted by four sister ecosystems, and the network grows from 5 active domains to 200 across 15 ETHOS and three regional federations. The domain-mapping skill scales because the domain contract is self-documenting: the dependencies element in each contract creates a navigable graph across the registry without requiring manual relationship tracking. When a new ETHOS is formed in the Costa Rica node, it creates its own local domains by following the same skill — the delegating body is the Costa Rica ETHOS's founding circle, the consent process is run locally, and the contracts are registered in both the local registry and the inter-ecosystem federation registry. Nested delegation manages the scale problem: the AE delegates authority to sub-circles, which become delegating bodies for their own sub-domains, keeping any single delegating body's domain count below fifteen — a manageable evaluation load. The 6-month evaluation cadence operates as a built-in garbage collection: domains whose purpose has been fulfilled or whose stewards have moved on are sunset rather than left to accumulate. At 200 domains, the registry surface shows 180 active, 8 under_review, 7 provisional, and 5 vacant — all statuses are visible, searchable, and addressable. No domains operate in shadow governance. An AI agent querying the registry for a participant's authority scope can traverse the dependency graph and determine within seconds whether a proposed action falls within any consented domain. The skill's structure — specifically the requirement that every domain be self-contained and cross-references be explicit — is what makes automated authority resolution possible at scale.

## §6. External Legal Pressure

The Indonesian government issues a regulatory requirement that co-living operations above a certain size must designate a "regulatory compliance officer" with authority to halt operations unilaterally if safety violations are detected. The authority structure this mandate describes — unilateral halt authority — is incompatible with NEOS consent principles. Rather than refuse the requirement or accept a governance structure that violates the ecosystem's principles, the AE routes the compliance function through the domain-mapping process. A "Regulatory Compliance" domain is drafted with the key responsibility of monitoring and reporting on legal compliance requirements for SHUR Bali operations. The constraints element is critical: "The Regulatory Compliance steward cannot halt operations unilaterally; safety concerns are raised immediately to the OSC and the affected domain stewards, and an emergency ACT process is triggered within 4 hours of any identified violation." The domain's deliverables include a compliance report to the relevant government body — the format the government requires. The steward's individual legal accountability under Indonesian law is noted in the domain's challenges element and in the steward's onboarding record. The government's requirement is satisfied: a designated person holds compliance responsibility and can be named in legal documents. The unilateral halt authority is structurally absent from the domain contract — the steward does not hold it, cannot exercise it, and the governance system does not recognize it. Legal jurisdiction applies to the individual in their personal legal capacity; NEOS governance structure is not a party to the regulatory relationship. The domain-mapping process separated the legitimate compliance function from the illegitimate authority demand.

## §7. Sudden Exit of 30%

Following a contentious OSC decision about OmniOne's expansion into a new region, seven of OmniOne's twenty-three active domain stewards resign simultaneously, citing loss of trust in the governance direction. Every domain they held enters "vacant" status immediately — seven domain contracts, previously active, now show status "vacant" in the registry. The relevant delegating bodies are notified automatically. Rather than paralysis, the system activates a structured triage. The AE convenes an emergency session within 48 hours. Each vacant domain is assessed against a criticality matrix: domains whose vacancy immediately blocks other domains' operations are prioritized. The Economics circle domain is critical — the funding request queue has four pending decisions. The Member Lifecycle domain is critical — three prospective members are mid-onboarding. The Community Engagement domain is non-critical in the short term; its deliverables are monthly and the next report is three weeks out. For critical domains, the delegating body immediately exercises temporary stewardship — the AE takes on Economics decisions for the urgent queue, with each decision documented as a delegating-body intervention in the amendment history. Two existing stewards with relevant competencies are proposed for emergency assignment through an accelerated consent round (24-hour advice window, compressed consent); both accept. For the non-critical domains, fast-track role-sunset evaluations are scheduled: can the Community Engagement responsibilities be absorbed by the AE general operations domain? If yes, a role-sunset process begins. The 30-day vacant window means no domain is permanently headless, no authority gap becomes a permanent governance structure, and the ecosystem's remaining seventeen stewards are not overwhelmed — only the highest-priority vacancies require immediate action.
