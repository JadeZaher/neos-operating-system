# Handover Checklist for Outgoing Stewards

**Purpose:** Ensure the incoming steward receives complete, actionable context.
Complete every section before the overlap period begins. The transfer cannot
finalize until this checklist is marked complete in the transfer record.

---

## 1. Pending Commitments

List every open commitment this role is currently responsible for.
For each item, state: what it is, its current status, who else is involved,
and the hard deadline if one exists.

- [ ] All in-progress deliverables documented with current status
- [ ] All scheduled decisions (upcoming consent rounds, advice windows) listed
- [ ] All unresolved escalations (GAIA levels) listed with escalation date and current level
- [ ] Any commitments where the steward has personally agreed to an obligation listed
- [ ] Any time-sensitive items in the next 30 days flagged clearly

---

## 2. Active Agreements Held

List every agreement where this role is listed as steward, party, or responsible party.

- [ ] Agreement ID and title for each active agreement
- [ ] Role in each agreement (steward / party / responsible party)
- [ ] Review dates for each agreement within the next 12 months
- [ ] Any agreements currently under review or amendment flagged
- [ ] Any agreements that the incoming steward will need to formally accept stewardship of

---

## 3. Decision Context

Explain the "why" behind current practices. This is institutional memory —
what the incoming steward cannot learn from documents alone.

- [ ] Why key current practices exist (decisions made, problems they solved)
- [ ] Background on any ongoing situations that look simple on paper but have history
- [ ] Context on any recent changes that have not yet been reflected in formal documents
- [ ] Known disagreements or tensions that are currently managed informally
- [ ] Any decisions that are pending but not yet formally entered into any process

---

## 4. Relationship Map

Document every significant relationship this role has with adjacent and dependent domains.

For each relationship:
- [ ] Domain name and current steward name
- [ ] What the relationship involves (dependency, collaboration, coordination point)
- [ ] Current dynamics (any tensions, outstanding negotiations, informal arrangements)
- [ ] How often contact typically occurs
- [ ] Any cross-AZPO contacts listed separately

---

## 5. Known Challenges

Identify the structural, interpersonal, and operational challenges the incoming steward will face.

- [ ] Structural challenges (constraints that are hard to work within, resource limitations)
- [ ] Interpersonal dynamics relevant to this role (not gossip — structural context)
- [ ] Operational complexity (systems that are hard to use, processes that are unclear)
- [ ] Any known gaps in the domain contract that do not reflect actual practice
- [ ] Any boundary disputes that are unresolved or likely to resurface

---

## 6. Upcoming Deadlines

List every deadline within the next 60 days that the incoming steward must be aware of.

- [ ] Agreement review dates
- [ ] Reporting or deliverable deadlines
- [ ] Scheduled governance sessions where this role has a specific responsibility
- [ ] External deadlines (legal, partner, or ecosystem-level)
- [ ] Evaluation schedule for this domain contract (next review date)

---

## 7. System Access Handover

List every system, account, or access credential this role holds.

For each item:
- [ ] System or account name
- [ ] Type of access (admin / edit / read)
- [ ] Transfer action: transferred to incoming steward / access revoked for outgoing / pending
- [ ] Who manages the transfer (outgoing steward / delegating body / system admin)

---

## Checklist Completion Sign-Off

The outgoing steward confirms this checklist is complete and accurate:

- [ ] All sections above are filled and reviewed
- [ ] No material information has been withheld
- [ ] The incoming steward has been given the opportunity to raise questions on each section
- [ ] All open items raised by the incoming steward have been either resolved or explicitly documented as accepted
- [ ] The handover document has been submitted to the delegating body
